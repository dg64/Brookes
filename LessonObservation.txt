During a lesson observation, you can focus on multiple dimensions of teaching and learning. Whether you're observing for professional development, evaluation, or training, here are the key areas to look at:

---

## 1. Lesson Planning & Structure

* Clear **learning objectives** (Are they shared with students?)
* Logical lesson flow (starter → main activity → plenary)
* Pacing (Is time managed effectively?)
* Alignment between objectives, activities, and assessment

---

## 2. Teaching Strategies

* Variety of instructional methods (discussion, modeling, group work, questioning)
* Clarity of explanations and instructions
* Use of examples and scaffolding
* Differentiation (support for struggling learners, stretch for advanced students)
* Use of questioning techniques (open vs. closed questions, wait time)

---

## 3. Student Engagement

* Level of participation (Are most students involved?)
* On-task behavior
* Enthusiasm or interest in the lesson
* Student collaboration and interaction
* Student responses to questions (depth of understanding)

---

## 4. Classroom Management

* Classroom routines and transitions
* Behavior management strategies
* Teacher presence and authority
* Respectful classroom environment
* Handling of disruptions

---

## 5. Assessment & Feedback

* Use of formative assessment (checking for understanding)
* Quality of teacher feedback (specific, actionable, timely)
* Opportunities for self- or peer-assessment
* Adjustment of teaching based on student responses

---

## 6. Learning Environment

* Physical layout of the classroom
* Use of resources (technology, textbooks, manipulatives)
* Inclusivity and equity
* Classroom culture (safe, supportive, encouraging)

---

## 7. Student Learning Evidence

* Are students meeting the learning objectives?
* Quality of student work
* Ability of students to explain their learning
* Progress within the lesson

---

## 8. Teacher–Student Relationships

* Tone and language used by the teacher
* Encouragement and praise
* Responsiveness to student needs
* Rapport and mutual respect

---

### If You’re Observing for Professional Development

You might also note:

* What worked particularly well
* Areas for growth
* Questions you’d ask the teacher afterward
* Specific examples (avoid vague comments)
